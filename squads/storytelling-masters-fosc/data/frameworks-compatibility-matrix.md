## Framework Pairs — Compatibility Analysis

### McKee "The Gap" ↔ Campbell "Monomyth"

**Relationship:** Complementary (different scales)

**Overlap:** Both address story structure, but at different granularities.

**Conflict Zone:**
- McKee focuses on **beat-by-beat, scene-level mechanics** (inciting incident, progressive complications, crisis, climax)
- Campbell focuses on **macro journey archetypes** (17 stages of departure/initiation/return)

**Integration Heuristic:**
- **Use McKee for:** Scene construction, beat sheets, ensuring every scene turns
- **Use Campbell for:** Overall arc design, finding archetypal resonance, hero transformation
- **Combined:** Campbell provides the map (17-stage journey), McKee provides the engine (how each stage turns on a beat)

**Example:** A keynote about personal transformation:
- Campbell identifies it as a "Road of Trials" story (stage 8-10 of Monomyth)
- McKee structures each trial as a scene with gap, turning point, value change

### Duarte "Sparkline" ↔ Dicks "5-Second Moment"

**Relationship:** Conflicting on opening strategy

**Overlap:** Both address presentation/story openings.

**Conflict:**
- **Duarte Sparkline:** Build contrast gradually — start with "what is" (status quo), then oscillate to "what could be"
- **Dicks "Start at the End":** Begin as close to the climax (5-second moment) as possible, then flashback

**Resolution Heuristic:**
- **Use Dicks for:** Personal stories <10min, emotional vulnerability, stage storytelling (Moth-style)
- **Use Duarte for:** Keynotes >18min, persuasive presentations with data, organizational change talks
- **Why:** Dicks optimizes for immediacy and emotion. Duarte optimizes for cognitive tension and call-to-action.

**Integration Pattern:** Can combine if you use Dicks' 5-second moment AS the first "what could be" peak in Duarte's Sparkline.

**Example:** Sales keynote:
- Open with Dicks: 5-second moment of transformation (flash-forward)
- Then Duarte: "But let me show you what is... and how we get to what could be"

### Miller "StoryBrand" ↔ Duarte "Sparkline"

**Relationship:** Conflicting on hero role (apparent), complementary when resolved

**Overlap:** Both say "customer/audience is the hero."

**Conflict (Apparent):**
- **Miller SB7:** Brand is the GUIDE, customer is the HERO (never make brand the hero)
- **Duarte:** Audience is the HERO, presenter is the MENTOR (same framework, different domain)

**Resolution:** NO CONFLICT — they're saying the same thing in different contexts.
- Miller → Marketing/brand messaging
- Duarte → Presentations/keynotes

**Integration Heuristic:**
- If creating **brand messaging:** Use Miller SB7 structure (7 elements: hero, problem, guide, plan, CTA, failure, success)
- If creating **presentation to deliver that message:** Use Duarte Sparkline to structure the talk

**Example:** Product launch:
- Miller SB7: Website copy positions customer as hero, product as guide
- Duarte Sparkline: Keynote oscillates "current state" vs "future with product" — presenter is mentor

### Heath "SUCCESs" ↔ Heinrichs "Figures of Speech"

**Relationship:** Potential conflict (simplicity vs ornamentation)

**Overlap:** Both address how to make messages memorable.

**Conflict:**
- **Heath SUCCESs SIMPLE:** "Core + compact" — strip to essence, avoid complexity
- **Heinrichs Figures:** Use chiasmus, antithesis, anaphora — add rhetorical ornamentation

**Resolution Heuristic:**
- **Simplicity FIRST, figures SECOND**
- Use Heath to find the core (1 sentence). Then use Heinrichs to EXPRESS that core memorably.
- Figures are the **container** for simplicity, not the enemy.

**Integration Pattern:**
1. Heath: Find the core ("X is Y because Z")
2. Heinrichs: Express it with a figure (chiasmus: "Ask not what your country can do for you...")

**Anti-Pattern:** Using figures to dress up a message that hasn't been simplified = "lipstick on a pig" (Duarte's phrase)

**Example:**
- Heath core: "Great ideas come from combinations, not isolation"
- Heinrichs figure (antithesis): "Innovation happens when minds collide, not when they hide"

### Cialdini "6 Principles" ↔ Heinrichs "Ethos/Pathos/Logos"

**Relationship:** Complementary (different frameworks, same goal)

**Overlap:** Both are persuasion systems.

**Difference:**
- **Heinrichs:** Classical rhetoric framework (character, emotion, logic)
- **Cialdini:** Behavioral psychology framework (reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity)

**Integration Heuristic:**
- **Heinrichs provides the STRUCTURE** (which appeal to use when: ethos for credibility, pathos for emotion, logos for proof)
- **Cialdini provides the TACTICS** (how to execute each appeal: authority = ethos, social proof = logos, scarcity = pathos)

**Mapping:**
| Heinrichs Appeal | Cialdini Principles |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Ethos (character)** | Authority, Liking |
| **Pathos (emotion)** | Scarcity (fear of loss), Reciprocity (gratitude) |
| **Logos (logic)** | Social Proof (everyone else agrees), Commitment/Consistency (you already agreed) |

**Example:** Persuading a team to adopt a new process:
- Heinrichs: Lead with Ethos (establish credibility) → Pathos (fear of falling behind) → Logos (data proves it works)
- Cialdini: Ethos via Authority (industry experts use it) → Pathos via Scarcity (limited beta slots) → Logos via Social Proof (5 teams already succeeded)

### McKee "Story Principles" ↔ Snyder "Save the Cat BS2"

**Relationship:** Complementary (philosophy vs formula)

**Overlap:** Both provide story structure.

**Difference:**
- **McKee:** Principle-driven (no formulas, deep analysis, beat-by-beat rigor)
- **Snyder:** Formula-driven (15 beats, plug-and-play, genre templates)

**McKee's Criticism of Snyder:** McKee would call BS2 a "paradigm" (which he rejects). "All notions of paradigms and foolproof story models for commercial success are nonsense." [SOURCE: Story, Introduction]

**When to Use Which:**
- **McKee:** When story is broken and you need diagnostic depth. When avoiding clichés. When crafting literary/prestige work.
- **Snyder:** When drafting fast (VSL, explainer video, genre screenplay). When structure is the blocker, not depth.

**Integration (Advanced):**
1. Use Snyder BS2 to get a **first draft structure** (15 beats)
2. Use McKee to **audit each beat**: Does it turn? Is there a gap? Does the value change?
3. Refine beats that fail McKee's tests

**Anti-Pattern:** Using Snyder as the final product without McKee's rigor = formulaic, predictable stories.

### Campbell "Monomyth" ↔ Snyder "10 Genres"

**Relationship:** Complementary (archetypal vs categorical)

**Overlap:** Both classify story types.

**Difference:**
- **Campbell Monomyth:** One universal pattern (hero's journey) that underlies ALL stories
- **Snyder 10 Genres:** 10 distinct story types (Monster in the House, Golden Fleece, Whydunit, etc.)

**Integration Heuristic:**
- **Campbell** tells you the **deep structure** (transformation arc, archetypal resonance)
- **Snyder** tells you the **surface genre** (audience expectations, plot mechanics)

**Example:** A story about a startup founder's journey:
- **Snyder Genre:** "Golden Fleece" (quest for treasure with a team)
- **Campbell Monomyth:** Hero's Journey (departure from corporate world, trials of fundraising, return with wisdom)

**Combined Use:**
1. Identify Snyder genre → sets audience expectations
2. Map to Campbell stages → ensures archetypal depth
3. Result: Genre-compliant story with mythic resonance

---

### Duarte "S.T.A.R. Moments" ↔ Heath "Power of Moments (EPIC)"

**Relationship:** Complementary (nearly identical frameworks)

**Overlap:** Both engineer defining moments.

**Difference:**
- **Duarte S.T.A.R.:** "Something They'll Always Remember" — memorable peaks in presentations
- **Heath EPIC:** Elevation, Pride, Insight, Connection — 4 elements of defining moments

**Integration:** These are the SAME concept applied to different domains:
- Duarte → Presentations
- Heath → Experiences (customer service, education, life events)

**Combined Use:**
- Use **Heath EPIC** to **design** the moment (what makes it defining?)
- Use **Duarte S.T.A.R.** to **deliver** it in a presentation (staging, pacing, reveal)

**Example:** Product demo in a keynote:
- Heath: Elevation (surprise demo of impossible feature) + Insight (audience realizes "this changes everything")
- Duarte: Stage it as the climax of the Sparkline oscillation, right before the call to action

## Framework Selection Decision Tree

**START HERE:**

### Q1: What's the primary goal?

- **Diagnose a broken story** → McKee "The Gap" + Story Principles
- **Structure a presentation** → Duarte Sparkline
- **Make an idea stick** → Heath SUCCESs
- **Persuade/argue** → Heinrichs Ethos/Pathos/Logos + Cialdini 6 Principles
- **Craft a personal story** → Dicks 5-Second Moment + Homework for Life
- **Brand messaging** → Miller StoryBrand SB7
- **Video/VSL script** → Snyder BS2 (15 beats)
- **Find archetypal depth** → Campbell Monomyth
- **Build audience connection** → Carnegie Public Speaking Fundamentals

---

### Q2: What's the time/format constraint?

- **<5 minutes (story)** → Dicks "Start at the End"
- **5-10 minutes (talk)** → Carnegie + Heath SUCCESs
- **18-20 minutes (TED-style)** → Gallo TED Mastery (9 Secrets)
- **30-60 minutes (keynote)** → Duarte Sparkline + Gallo
- **90+ minutes (workshop)** → Carnegie + Heath Power of Moments

---

### Q3: What's the content type?

- **Pure narrative (fiction-style)** → McKee + Campbell + Snyder
- **Data-driven presentation** → Duarte + Heath (Concrete)
- **Persuasive argument** → Heinrichs + Cialdini
- **Personal story** → Dicks + McKee (The Gap for structure)
- **Marketing copy** → Miller SB7 + Heath SUCCESs

## Anti-Patterns (Framework Mismatches)

### ❌ Using Snyder BS2 for Literary Fiction
**Why:** BS2 optimizes for commercial genre films. Literary work needs McKee's depth and rejection of formulas.

### ❌ Using Campbell Monomyth for Comedy
**Why:** Monomyth is about transformation. Pure comedy (no growth arc) doesn't fit — use Snyder's "Fool Triumphant" genre instead.

### ❌ Using Duarte Sparkline for Audiences Who Already Agree
**Why:** Sparkline tension = "what is" vs "what could be." If audience is converted, there's no tension. Use McKee (internal conflict) or Dicks (vulnerability) instead.

### ❌ Using Heath SUCCESs Without Source Credibility
**Why:** Sticky ≠ believed. If audience distrusts you (low ethos), ideas stick as "propaganda." Add Heinrichs Ethos or Cialdini Authority FIRST.

### ❌ Using Miller SB7 for Complex B2B (Multiple Stakeholders)
**Why:** SB7 assumes single hero. B2B often has 5+ decision-makers with different problems. Use McKee's "4 levels of conflict" to address each stakeholder layer.

## Conflict Resolution Protocols

### When Two Experts Give Contradictory Advice

**Protocol:**
1. **Identify the domain difference** — Are they solving the same problem or different ones?
2. **Check the time scale** — One might address beats, the other acts
3. **Apply "Both/And" thinking** — Can they layer (Campbell + McKee)?
4. **Escalate to orchestrator** — storytelling-masters-chief resolves conflicts via multi-expert consultation

**Example Conflict:**
- **McKee:** "No scene that doesn't turn"
- **Dicks:** "Use long stakes-free sections to build empathy"

**Resolution:** McKee is talking about STORY scenes (fiction, film). Dicks is talking about PERSONAL STORY performance (5-10min stage). Different formats, both valid in their domain.
