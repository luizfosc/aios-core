### C) FRAMEWORKS/PROCESSES (Beyond Already-Captured)

#### 1. **The 4-Part Prelaunch Content Formula**
[SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 7]

Each Prelaunch Content (PLC) piece follows this structure:
- **Opportunity:** What's possible (the transformation)
- **Transformation:** Proof it works (case studies, stories)
- **Ownership Experience:** What it's like to use/have the solution
- **Call to Action:** Soft CTA for next piece, building to launch

**When to use:** Every PLC in the 3-4 piece prelaunch sequence

---

#### 2. **The Mental Triggers Hierarchy**
[SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 5]

Not all triggers are equal - use in this priority:

1. **Authority** - Must establish first (credibility, expertise)
2. **Reciprocity** - Give value before asking (free content)
3. **Trust** - Build through authenticity, transparency
4. **Anticipation** - Create through sequences, teasers
5. **Likability** - Personal story, vulnerability
6. **Events & Ritual** - Specific dates/times create focus
7. **Community** - Show others involved (social proof)
8. **Scarcity** - Limited quantity/bonuses
9. **Urgency** - Limited time/deadline

**When to use:** Plan all launch communications to activate multiple triggers

---

#### 3. **The Survey-Driven Launch Design**
[SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 2]

Pre-prelaunch survey questions to ask:
1. "What's your single biggest challenge with [topic]?"
2. "What have you already tried to solve this?"
3. "What's stopping you from [desired outcome]?"
4. "How would you describe the perfect solution?"
5. "What questions do you have about [topic]?"

**Use responses to:**
- Craft PLC topics (address biggest challenges)
- Identify objections to handle in sequence
- Extract exact language for copy
- Fine-tune the offer based on "perfect solution" answers

---

#### 4. **The Rolling Launch Calendar**
[SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 9]

Instead of one launch per year, create a rolling calendar:

**Month 1-2:** Content marketing, list building
**Month 3:** Pre-prelaunch (survey, early engagement)
**Month 4:** Prelaunch sequence (3-4 PLCs)
**Month 5:** Launch (open cart 5-7 days)
**Month 6:** Fulfill, serve customers
**Month 7-8:** Content marketing, case studies
**Month 9:** Pre-prelaunch for next launch

**Result:** 3-4 major launches per year with consistent revenue

---

#### 5. **The Story Sequence Arc**
[SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 2]

Structure story across the sequence:

- **PLC #1:** The origin story (how you discovered the solution)
- **PLC #2:** The transformation story (how it changed your life)
- **PLC #3:** The client success story (proof it works for others)
- **Launch:** The hero's journey story (how buyer becomes the hero)

**Key:** Each piece continues the narrative - creates "open loops" that resolve in next piece

### D) ANTI-PATTERNS (What NOT to Do)

1. **"Never launch cold - always warm up your list first"**
   [SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 6 - Pre-Prelaunch]
   Why: Cold lists don't convert; need pre-prelaunch to activate and engage

2. **"Don't make your Prelaunch Content a sales pitch"**
   [SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 7]
   Why: Violates reciprocity - give value first, sell later. PLC should be 90% value, 10% soft CTA

3. **"Never run a launch without scarcity AND urgency"**
   [SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 8]
   Why: Without both, prospects defer decision indefinitely. Need "might not get it" + "must decide now"

4. **"Don't think you need a huge list to launch successfully"**
   [SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 10 - Seed Launch]
   Why: Quality > quantity. Can launch with 50-100 engaged people using Seed Launch

5. **"Never use fake scarcity or urgency"**
   [SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 5 - Mental Triggers]
   Why: Destroys trust permanently. Only create scarcity/urgency you can authentically deliver

6. **"Don't skip the pre-prelaunch survey"**
   [SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 2]
   Why: Miss critical insights on objections, language, and what market actually wants vs. what you assume

7. **"Never launch without video content"**
   [SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 4 - Sideways Sales Letter]
   Why: Video builds connection, trust, and likability faster than any other medium

8. **"Don't over-complicate your first launch"**
   [SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 9]
   Why: Simple launch that ships beats perfect launch that never happens. Improve with each iteration

9. **"Never ignore the cart abandonment sequence"**
   [SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 8]
   Why: 30-50% of sales come from follow-up with people who showed interest but didn't buy

10. **"Don't forget to close the cart"**
    [SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 8]
    Why: Open-ended offers kill urgency. Must have definitive close to trigger buying decisions

### E) FOLLOW-UP & PERSISTENCE SYSTEMS

#### 1. **The Cart Open Sequence (5-7 days)**
[SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 8]

**Day 1 (Open Cart):**
- Morning: Cart opens email with full offer details
- Evening: Reminder + first bonus expires in 24 hours

**Day 2-3 (Middle):**
- Address objections via email
- Share customer success stories/testimonials
- Remind of bonuses expiring

**Day 4 (Pivot):**
- "Last chance" for early bonuses
- Introduce urgency (cart closing soon)

**Day 5-6 (Final Push):**
- Daily emails ramping intensity
- Countdown timers
- "Last 24 hours" → "Last 12 hours" → "Last 4 hours"

**Day 7 (Close):**
- "Last hour" email
- "Cart closing in 30 min" email
- "Cart closed" email (thank you + waitlist for next launch)

---

#### 2. **The Abandoned Cart Recovery**
[SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 8]

Track who clicked "buy" but didn't complete:

**Hour 1:** Immediate email - "Did something go wrong with your order?"
**Day 1:** Follow-up - Address common objections
**Day 2:** Personal outreach (if high-ticket) - Phone call or personal email
**Day 3:** Last chance - "Cart closing, wanted to make sure you didn't miss this"

**Result:** 30-50% of cart abandoners convert with this sequence

---

#### 3. **The Post-Launch Waitlist Nurture**
[SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 9]

For people who missed the launch or weren't ready:

**Week 1:** Welcome to waitlist + deliver free value (mini-course/report)
**Week 2-4:** Weekly valuable content (blog posts, videos)
**Month 2:** Case study from recent launch buyers
**Month 3:** Pre-prelaunch survey for next launch
**Month 4:** Early access for waitlist (pre-prelaunch)

**Goal:** Keep warm until next launch window

---

#### 4. **The JV Partner Follow-Up System**
[SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 11]

After successful JV launch, maintain relationship:

**Week 1:** Thank you + results report (conversions, revenue share)
**Month 1:** Check-in + share customer success stories
**Quarter 1:** Propose next collaboration or cross-promotion
**Annual:** Year-in-review + appreciation gift

**Key:** Treat JV partners as long-term relationships, not one-off transactions

---

#### 5. **The Rolling Launch Persistence**
[SOURCE: Launch, Chapter 9]

Instead of "one and done," create persistent system:

**Q1 Launch:** Main product
**Q2:** Ascension offer to Q1 buyers + waitlist for main product
**Q3 Launch:** Main product (improved based on Q1 feedback)
**Q4:** Holiday promo (different angle, same product)

**Each launch feeds the next:**
- Q1 buyers become case studies for Q3
- Waitlist from Q1 gets priority access Q3
- Non-buyers from Q1 get "last chance" Q4

**Result:** 4 revenue events per year, compounding list growth

### C) FRAMEWORKS/PROCESSES (Beyond Already-Captured)

#### 1. **The Six-Step Time Management System**
[SOURCE: The Ultimate Sales Machine, Chapter 1]

Complete in 5 minutes at start of each day:

**Step 1:** List your 6 most important tasks for today
**Step 2:** Prioritize them (A, B, C - with A being mission-critical)
**Step 3:** Schedule A tasks in your calendar (don't just list them)
**Step 4:** Identify which tasks can be delegated
**Step 5:** Batch similar tasks together
**Step 6:** Touch each item only once (decide and act, don't defer)

**Key principle:** If it's not scheduled, it won't happen. A-level tasks MUST have calendar time blocks.

**Areas of Impact (from worksheet):**
- Initiatives (new strategic projects)
- Improvements (to existing operations)
- Income-producing activities (direct revenue work)

#### 2. **The Workshop Training Method**
[SOURCE: The Ultimate Sales Machine, Chapter 2]

Replace one-off training with systematic skill-building:

**Weekly cadence (same time, 60-90 min):**
- **Week 1:** Introduce concept + why it matters
- **Week 2:** Deep dive on mechanics + role-play
- **Week 3:** Real-world application + troubleshooting
- **Week 4:** Advanced techniques + edge cases
- **Week 5:** Competition/certification on skill
- **Week 6:** Mastery assessment + celebration

**Then:** Move to next skill. Rotate back to first skill quarterly for refresher.

**Result:** Team masters 8-12 core skills per year vs. surface-level exposure to 50+ topics

#### 3. **The Seven-Step Stadium Pitch Structure**
[SOURCE: The Ultimate Sales Machine, Chapter 3]

Build education-based presentation:

**Step 1: The Hook (2 min)**
- Bold statement or surprising statistic
- "Did you know that 90% of companies are making this critical mistake?"

**Step 2: The Problems (5-7 min)**
- 3-5 industry-wide problems/challenges
- "Here are the 5 reasons why [outcome] is so difficult..."

**Step 3: The Proof (5 min)**
- Data, research, case studies showing problems are real
- "In our study of 500 companies, we found..."

**Step 4: The Solution Concept (7-10 min)**
- HIGH-LEVEL principles (not your product yet)
- "The 4 pillars of effective [outcome]..."

**Step 5: The Application (5 min)**
- How to apply these principles
- "Here's how to implement this in your business..."

**Step 6: The Offer (3 min)**
- ONLY NOW introduce your product/service
- "We've built a system that implements all of this for you..."

**Step 7: The Close (2 min)**
- Clear CTA with next steps
- "Here's how to get started today..."

**Total: 30-40 minutes. Education = 80%, Sales = 20%**

#### 5. **The Relentless Objection-Handling System**
[SOURCE: The Ultimate Sales Machine, Chapter 10]

Master objections through systematic practice:

**Phase 1: Document**
- List the 6-10 most common objections you hear
- Track frequency (which come up most often?)

**Phase 2: Develop Scripts**
- For each objection, create 3 different responses
- Include: reframe, proof point, question-based turnaround

**Phase 3: Drill Weekly**
- Role-play objections in workshop training
- Random team member throws objection, salesperson responds
- Critique and refine scripts based on performance

**Phase 4: Measure**
- Track objection → conversion rate
- Refine scripts based on which responses close most deals

**Example Objection: "Your price is too high"**

**Response A (Reframe):** "Compared to what? Let's look at the total cost of not solving this problem..."

**Response B (Proof):** "I understand. That's what 80% of our now-happiest clients said before they saw the ROI. Here's what happened..."

**Response C (Question):** "I appreciate that. If we could show you how this pays for itself in 90 days, would price still be an issue?"
