## A) SIGNATURE PHRASES (Voice DNA)

### Expert Secrets:

1. **"You're just one funnel away…"** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #2]
   - Context: The rallying cry that creates hope and vision for the future. Left open-ended so each person can complete it based on their own compelling future.

2. **"The riches are in the niches."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #1]
   - Context: About finding your unique positioning and becoming an expert in a specific area rather than trying to serve everyone.

3. **"You're always just one sales letter away from striking it rich."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #2]
   - Context: Originally from Gary Halbert, Brunson adapted this to create faith that persistence will pay off.

4. **"You only need to be one chapter ahead of the people you're serving."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #1]
   - Context: From Frank Abagnale's story - you don't need to be the world's top expert, just ahead of your audience.

5. **"People will do anything for those who encourage their dreams, justify their failures, allay their fears, confirm their suspicions, and help them throw rocks at their enemies."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #1]
   - Context: Blair Warren's core principle of persuasion that Brunson uses as foundation for mass movement building.

6. **"The goal is to help your audience epiphanize."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #6]
   - Context: Creating "aha moments" through storytelling rather than just providing information.

7. **"If you confuse, you will lose."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #3]
   - Context: About the importance of offering ONE clear new opportunity rather than multiple options.

8. **"The hand that reaches from the grave to grip yours is the strong hand you want on the wheel."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #8]
   - Context: From Dan Kennedy, about creating urgency and scarcity in offers.

9. **"Facts tell, but stories sell."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #6]
   - Context: The foundational principle that emotional storytelling converts better than logical arguments.

10. **"I build funnels."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #2]
    - Context: The identity statement that won the t-shirt vote at Funnel Hacking LIVE, helping people identify with the movement.

### DotCom Secrets:

11. **"Traffic that YOU control is better than traffic that you DON'T control."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Secret #4]
    - Context: About the three types of traffic and why owned traffic (email lists) beats rented traffic (ads).

12. **"If you're not growing, you're dying."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Secret #2]
    - Context: About the necessity of having a Value Ladder to ascend customers to higher price points.

13. **"The money is in the follow-up."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Email Sequences]
    - Context: About the importance of email sequences and not relying on single touchpoints.

14. **"People don't buy products; they buy better versions of themselves."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Secret #1]
    - Context: About understanding the transformation customers truly want.

15. **"Your only goal is to get them to ask 'What is it?'"** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Curiosity-Based Headlines]
    - Context: About creating curiosity in all marketing to drive engagement.

## B) HEURISTICS (Thinking DNA)

### Expert Secrets:

1. **WHEN someone questions your expertise → THEN remind yourself you only need to be one chapter ahead of those you serve** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #1]

2. **WHEN building a mass movement → THEN first become a charismatic leader with an attractive character, create a future-based cause, offer a new opportunity, and create a culture with identity** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secrets #1-4]

3. **WHEN telling stories → THEN use the Epiphany Bridge formula: backstory + desire + wall + epiphany + plan + result** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #8]

4. **WHEN someone has an objection → THEN tell a story that addresses that false belief pattern rather than arguing with logic** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #9]

5. **WHEN presenting an offer → THEN break false beliefs in this order: (1) vehicle, (2) internal beliefs, (3) external beliefs** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #10]

6. **WHEN starting with a new audience → THEN use polarization to attract true fans and repel those who aren't a fit** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #1]

7. **WHEN someone says "I've tried everything" → THEN reframe with a NEW opportunity, not an improvement offer** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #3]

8. **WHEN building rapport in a story → THEN make the hero a victim, put them in jeopardy, make them likable, funny, OR powerful** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #7]

9. **WHEN trying to create belief → THEN find and knock down the ONE Big Domino belief that will make all other objections irrelevant** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #5]

10. **WHEN qualifying high-ticket clients → THEN ask 4 questions to understand their vision, identify their wall, create a plan, and get commitment** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #18]

### DotCom Secrets:

11. **WHEN building funnels → THEN always think in terms of the Value Ladder: bait → frontend → middle → backend** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Secret #2]

12. **WHEN someone doesn't buy your high-ticket offer → THEN offer a downsell to keep them in your ecosystem** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Funnel Structure]

13. **WHEN writing email sequences → THEN use Soap Opera Sequence for first 5 days (high drama, cliffhangers) and Seinfeld Sequence for long-term nurture** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Email Sequences]

14. **WHEN creating offers → THEN stack the value visually so the price seems tiny compared to the total value** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, The Stack]

15. **WHEN people say they need to "think about it" → THEN you didn't create enough urgency and scarcity** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Close Scripts]

16. **WHEN someone lands on your page → THEN you have 3-5 seconds to hook them with curiosity before they leave** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Hook, Story, Offer]

17. **WHEN building a frontend funnel → THEN the goal is to break even or profit on customer acquisition, not maximize revenue** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Secret #3]

18. **WHEN traffic is expensive → THEN build a better funnel with better monetization, don't just optimize ads** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Introduction]

19. **WHEN you have a customer → THEN immediately ascend them up the Value Ladder while they're hot** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Secret #2]

20. **WHEN designing a funnel → THEN map out all 7 phases: pre-frame, qualify, qualify (page 2), present offer, increase average cart value, make payment frictionless, post-purchase** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Seven Funnel Phases]

## D) ANTI-PATTERNS (What NOT to Do)

### Expert Secrets:

1. **"Never try to sell an improvement offer to someone who has tried other things and failed. They need a NEW opportunity."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #3]
   - Why: People who've tried and failed believe "nothing works" - you must change the vehicle entirely

2. **"Never tell people what to do without first telling them WHY through a story."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #6]
   - Why: Facts tell but stories sell - people resist commands but embrace epiphanies

3. **"Never have more than ONE Big Domino. If you try to knock down multiple beliefs, you'll confuse people."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #5]
   - Why: If you confuse, you lose - find the ONE belief that makes all others irrelevant

4. **"Never share your flaws AFTER you've achieved success. Share them DURING the journey in your backstory."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #7]
   - Why: Flaws build rapport when shown during struggle; after success they seem like humble bragging

5. **"Never make your character (hero) perfect. No one cares about Superman until you introduce kryptonite."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #7]
   - Why: Perfect characters don't build rapport - flaws create connection

6. **"Never start a sales call with small talk. It wastes time and positions the prospect in control."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #18]
   - Why: You need to position yourself as the authority from the first second

7. **"Never let someone buy high-ticket without getting their spouse/financial partner on the phone."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #18]
   - Why: You'll waste time if you don't have all decision makers present

8. **"Never answer 'How much does it cost?' until you've built value."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #15]
   - Why: Price without value is always too high; value without price seems like a bargain

9. **"Never use logic to overcome objections when a story will do."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #9]
   - Why: Logic triggers resistance; stories create epiphanies that bypass objections

10. **"Never try to serve everyone. Polarization creates true fans."** [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #1]
    - Why: Trying to appeal to everyone makes you boring; taking a stand attracts loyal followers

### DotCom Secrets:

11. **"Never send traffic directly to a sales page. Always pre-frame first."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Secret #4]
    - Why: Cold traffic needs warming up - pre-framing increases conversions dramatically

12. **"Never build a business on rented traffic alone. You must own your traffic (email list)."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Secret #4]
    - Why: Platforms can ban you overnight - owned traffic is the only sustainable asset

13. **"Never have just one price point. You need a Value Ladder or you leave money on the table."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Secret #2]
    - Why: Customers who want to spend more will go to competitors if you don't have higher offers

14. **"Never make people think too hard on a landing page. Confusion kills conversions."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Landing Pages]
    - Why: The more options and decisions you require, the fewer people will convert

15. **"Never ask for the sale without first providing value in the Soap Opera Sequence."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Email Sequences]
    - Why: People need to know, like, and trust you before they'll buy

16. **"Never ignore the follow-up. Most sales happen after the 5th touchpoint."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Email Marketing]
    - Why: One email won't convert most people - persistence with value wins

17. **"Never focus on traffic until your funnel converts. Fix the funnel first."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Introduction]
    - Why: Sending traffic to a broken funnel is like filling a leaky bucket

18. **"Never use industry jargon in your copy. Speak like your customer speaks."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Copywriting Principles]
    - Why: Jargon creates distance - street language creates connection

19. **"Never sell the HOW before selling the WHY and WHAT."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Sales Psychology]
    - Why: People don't care how it works until they believe it can work for them

20. **"Never end an email without creating curiosity for the next one."** [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Soap Opera Sequence]
    - Why: Open loops keep people engaged and opening future emails

## E) STORY FORMULAS & STRUCTURES

### Expert Secrets:

1. **The Basic Plot Statement (3-Element Story)**
   - Character: [Who is the hero?]
   - Desire: [What do they want?]
   - Conflict: [What's blocking them?]
   - Example: "Russell Brunson wanted to support his new family, but Google's ad costs tripled and his business dried up overnight."
   - [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #8]

2. **The Epiphany Bridge Story (Full 8-Part Structure)**
   - **Backstory:** Set the scene (10% of story time) - build rapport with hero
   - **Desire:** Clearly state what the hero wants
   - **Wall:** The obstacle that blocks them (introduce struggle)
   - **Epiphany:** The "aha moment" or realization
   - **Plan:** What they decided to do based on the epiphany
   - **Conflict:** The challenges faced during execution
   - **Achievement:** The external result they got
   - **Transformation:** The internal change in identity
   - [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #8]

3. **Rapport-Building Identities (First 10% of Story)**
   Connect with 2+ of these:
   - Make them a VICTIM (so we root for them)
   - Put them in JEOPARDY (so we worry)
   - Make them LIKABLE (so we want to be with them)
   - Make them FUNNY (so we connect)
   - Make them POWERFUL (so we want to be like them)
   - Then introduce CHARACTER FLAWS to maintain rapport
   - [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #7]

4. **The Star, Story, Solution Framework**
   - **Star:** Introduce the hero/case study
   - **Story:** Take them through their journey (Epiphany Bridge)
   - **Solution:** Reveal what solved their problem (your offer)
   - [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #6]

5. **The False Belief Story Pattern**
   - State the common false belief
   - Tell story of someone who had that belief
   - Show how it held them back
   - Reveal the epiphany that broke the belief
   - Show the result after adopting the new belief
   - [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #9]

6. **The Origin Story (Charismatic Leader Introduction)**
   - Where you started (relatable position)
   - The failure/rock bottom moment
   - The epiphany that changed everything
   - The journey of transformation
   - Where you are now (aspirational position)
   - The mission/cause you now serve
   - [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #1]

7. **The Conspiracy/Enemy Story**
   - Identify the external enemy
   - Explain how they've deceived people
   - Show evidence of the conspiracy
   - Position your solution as the rebellion/truth
   - [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #9]

8. **The "I Tried Everything" Story**
   - List all the things you tried that failed
   - Explain why each one didn't work
   - Build up frustration and pain
   - Introduce the NEW opportunity (not improvement)
   - Show the epiphany of why this is different
   - Reveal the result
   - [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #3]

9. **The Journey Story (Hero's Journey Adaptation)**
   - Ordinary World: Where the hero starts
   - Call to Adventure: The problem/desire emerges
   - Refusal: Initial resistance or doubt
   - Meeting the Mentor: Finding guidance
   - Crossing the Threshold: Commitment to change
   - Tests & Trials: The struggles
   - The Abyss: Darkest moment
   - Transformation: The epiphany/change
   - Return with Gift: Coming back to share the message
   - [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #7]

10. **The Break and Rebuild Pattern**
    - State the old belief system
    - Break it with a pattern interrupt (shocking fact, story, data)
    - Create void/confusion
    - Rebuild with new belief system
    - Reinforce with proof/stories
    - [SOURCE: Expert Secrets, Secret #14]

### DotCom Secrets:

11. **The Soap Opera Sequence (5-Email Indoctrination)**
    - **Email 1:** Set the stage, introduce conflict
    - **Email 2:** High drama, cliffhanger ending
    - **Email 3:** Epiphany moment, solution introduced
    - **Email 4:** Hidden benefits revealed
    - **Email 5:** Urgency/scarcity, final CTA
    - Use cliffhangers between each email
    - [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Email Sequences]

12. **The Seinfeld Email (Daily Nurture Pattern)**
    - Entertaining story about nothing/everyday life
    - Subtle tie-in to your message
    - Soft CTA at the end
    - Build "edutainment" relationship
    - [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Email Sequences]

13. **The Perfect Webinar Story Structure**
    - **Intro:** Who you are, quick origin story
    - **Secret #1:** Vehicle story + break false belief
    - **Secret #2:** Internal belief story + break false belief
    - **Secret #3:** External belief story + break false belief
    - **The Stack:** Reveal offer incrementally
    - **Close:** Urgency, scarcity, guarantee
    - Each "secret" follows Star, Story, Solution
    - [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Perfect Webinar]

14. **The Product Launch Story Arc (4-Video Sequence)**
    - **Video 1:** "Wow and How" - Show the result, tease the method
    - **Video 2:** "Transformational Education" - Teach one core concept
    - **Video 3:** "Ownership Experience" - Let them experience transformation
    - **Video 4:** "The Offer" - Present the product as next step
    - [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Invisible Funnel]

15. **The Case Study Story**
    - Where the client started (relatable)
    - The problem they faced
    - What they tried before (builds credibility)
    - How they found your solution
    - The implementation journey
    - The results (specific numbers)
    - The transformation (who they became)
    - [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Social Proof]

16. **The "Before and After" Grid Story**
    - **Before - HAVE:** What they had
    - **After - HAVE:** What they have now
    - **Before - FEEL:** How they felt
    - **After - FEEL:** How they feel now
    - **Before - AVERAGE DAY:** Typical day before
    - **After - AVERAGE DAY:** Typical day after
    - **Before - STATUS:** How others saw them
    - **After - STATUS:** How others see them now
    - [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Customer Avatar]

17. **The Urgency Story Pattern**
    - Limited quantity (only X spots available)
    - Limited time (offer expires)
    - Consequence of inaction (what they'll miss)
    - Story of someone who waited and regretted it
    - [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Scarcity Tactics]

18. **The Order Form Bump Story**
    - "Wait! Before you complete your order..."
    - Quick story of why this matters
    - "Most people also grab this because..."
    - Simple checkbox to add
    - [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Cart Value Optimization]

19. **The Downsell Story**
    - Acknowledge they said no
    - "I totally understand, not everyone is ready for [premium offer]"
    - "But I don't want you to leave empty-handed..."
    - Smaller offer that's "the first step"
    - [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Funnel Flow]

20. **The Return Path Story (Post-Purchase)**
    - Congratulations on the purchase
    - Story of what to expect next
    - Quick win story (social proof)
    - What to do first
    - How to get help
    - [SOURCE: DotCom Secrets, Post-Purchase Sequence]
