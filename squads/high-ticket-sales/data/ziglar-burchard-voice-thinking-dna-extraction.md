## B) HEURISTICS (Thinking DNA - Decision Rules)

### Zig Ziglar - Secrets of Closing the Sale

1. WHEN prospect says "I need to think about it" → THEN use the "Bride Close": agree with them, create urgency by highlighting external timing factors (seasonal discounts, inventory limitations, tax benefits), not internal pressure [SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter 1]

2. WHEN prospect raises price objection → THEN break down cost per day/use (the "1902 Close" - divide total cost by years of use, then days, to show pennies per day) [SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Imagination]

3. WHEN prospect says "I want to shop around" → THEN acknowledge it's smart, then ask specific comparison questions to uncover what they're really looking for [SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Objections]

4. WHEN making a sales call → THEN ask "Did I catch you at a busy time, or do you have [specific number] minutes to talk?" - shows respect and gets commitment [SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Timing]

5. WHEN prospect criticizes previous purchase → THEN validate their decision at the time: "At the time you made this decision, under the circumstances which existed at that moment and with the information you had, I'm confident most of us would have made the same decision" [SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Making Prospect Feel Good]

6. WHEN facing "no hurry" objection → THEN create urgency with external factors (limited inventory, seasonal benefits, pending price increases), never internal pressure [SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter 1]

7. WHEN prospect shows buying signals (nodding, handling product, asking detailed questions) → THEN use a trial close immediately - don't wait for entire presentation [SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Trial Close]

8. WHEN selling to couples and one is hesitant → THEN lower voice, look hesitant spouse in eye, and say "Worse than [spouse] wants it, [spouse] wants YOU to want [spouse] to have it" [SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Husband-Wife Closes]

9. WHEN competitor has existing relationship → THEN position yourself as willing to "try harder" because you must EARN the business while competitor takes it for granted [SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Competitive Situations]

10. WHEN using testimonials → THEN match the testimonial to the specific objection raised, highlight key points, and use format "I know how you feel, [Name] felt the same way, here's what he found..." [SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Testimonials]

## C) FRAMEWORKS/PROCESSES (Not Already Captured)

### Zig Ziglar - Named Closing Techniques Catalog

#### 1. The "Assumptive Close"
**Description:** Assume the sale is made and begin discussing delivery, installation, or next steps
**When to use:** When prospect has shown strong buying signals throughout presentation
**Example:** "Now, Mr. Prospect, would Wednesday or Thursday be better for delivery?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Closing Techniques]

#### 2. The "Alternative Choice Close" (or "Either/Or Close")
**Description:** Give prospect choice between two yeses, never yes/no
**When to use:** When moving from presentation to commitment
**Example:** "Would you prefer the blue or the red? Cash or financing? Delivery Tuesday or Friday?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Closing Techniques]

#### 3. The "Summary Close" (or "Ben Franklin Close")
**Description:** Create two columns - reasons to buy vs. reasons not to buy, walk through each point
**When to use:** When prospect is analytical and needs logical justification
**Example:** Draw line down paper, list benefits on left, objections on right, address each
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Logical Closes]

#### 4. The "1-2-3 Close"
**Description:** Break complex decision into three simple steps/questions
**When to use:** When prospect is overwhelmed by decision complexity
**Example:** "Three questions: 1) Do you like it? 2) Do you want it? 3) Can you afford the small investment?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Simplification]

#### 5. The "Bride Close" (Urgency without Pressure)
**Description:** Agree prospect doesn't need to hurry, then introduce external timing factors
**When to use:** "I'm in no hurry" objection
**Example:** "You're right, you don't have to decide today. But the tax year ends in 60 days, and after that this benefit is gone for another year."
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter 1]

#### 6. The "Cost Close" (or "Reduction to Ridiculous")
**Description:** Break total cost down to cost per day/use to make investment seem minimal
**When to use:** Price objection on durable/long-lasting products
**Example:** "$1,500 seems like a lot, but over 30 years that's 50 cents per day - less than a cup of coffee - for something you'll use every single day"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Price Objections]

#### 7. The "Trial Close"
**Description:** Test prospect's readiness to buy before completing full presentation
**When to use:** When prospect shows buying signals (nodding, handling product, asking detailed questions)
**Example:** "If the machine had only this one blade, how many of you have already decided you'd like to have one? Can I see your hands?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter 13]

#### 8. The "Companion Close"
**Description:** Use one prospect's decision to influence another present
**When to use:** When multiple prospects are present and one commits
**Example:** "Just because you didn't have the chance to get it ten years ago doesn't mean you should continue to deny yourself, does it?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Group Sales]

#### 9. The "Physical Action Close"
**Description:** Begin filling out paperwork while still discussing - physically moving toward close
**When to use:** When verbal agreement is reached but prospect hesitates on commitment
**Example:** Start writing order, ask for spelling of name, address - makes backing out harder
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Momentum]

#### 10. The "Testimonial Close"
**Description:** Use third-party validation at the moment of objection
**When to use:** When prospect raises concern about service, quality, reliability
**Example:** "I know how you feel. John at XYZ Company felt the same way. Here's what he found..." [show letter]
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Testimonials]

#### 11. The "Pressure Belief Close" (The John Nevin Story)
**Description:** Persist not from desire to make sale, but from BELIEF product will genuinely help prospect
**When to use:** When you deeply believe prospect NEEDS what you sell
**Example:** The German immigrant story - John Nevin stayed until midnight because he BELIEVED the encyclopedia would change their son's life, and it did
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter 35 - "Tank You, Young Man"]

#### 12. The "Shame Close" (Use with Extreme Caution)
**Description:** Gently challenge prospect's self-image or values when they conflict with decision
**When to use:** RARELY - only when prospect's stated values conflict with their hesitation
**Example:** "You said your children's education is your top priority. Doesn't it make sense to invest in tools that support that priority?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Emotional Closes]

#### 13. The "Negative Reverse Close"
**Description:** When prospect is resistant, appear to withdraw the offer
**When to use:** When prospect seems to want product but enjoys the back-and-forth
**Example:** "You know, Mr. Prospect, I'm not sure this is right for you after all. Let me ask you..."
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Psychology]

#### 14. The "Puppy Dog Close"
**Description:** Let prospect take product home on trial (like puppy from pet store - they won't return it)
**When to use:** When confident in product quality and prospect's hesitation is fear of unknown
**Example:** "Take it home for the weekend. If you don't love it, I'll pick it up Monday, no charge."
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Trial Strategies]

### Zig Ziglar - The Four Reasons Prospects Don't Buy

**Framework:** Every "no" falls into one of four categories. Identify which, then use appropriate close.

1. **No Need** - Prospect doesn't see the need for your product
   - **Solution:** Uncover latent needs through questions, demonstrate problems they don't know they have
   
2. **No Money** - Prospect claims they can't afford it
   - **Solution:** Cost Close (reduction to ridiculous), payment plans, ROI demonstration, priority shifting
   
3. **No Hurry** - Prospect sees no urgency to buy NOW
   - **Solution:** Bride Close, external timing factors, limited availability, seasonal benefits
   
4. **No Trust** - Prospect doesn't trust you, company, or product
   - **Solution:** Testimonials, guarantees, third-party validation, credibility building

[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter 1]

### Zig Ziglar - The Objection Handling Formula

**Framework:** When prospect raises objection, follow this exact sequence:

1. **HEAR IT OUT** - Never interrupt, let them finish completely
2. **FEED IT BACK** - "If I understand correctly, you're concerned about..."
3. **QUESTION IT** - Ask clarifying questions to understand real objection
4. **ANSWER IT** - Address the real concern (which may be different from stated concern)
5. **CONFIRM IT** - "Does that answer your concern?" (Get explicit yes)
6. **CONTINUE** - Move forward assuming objection is resolved

[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Objection Handling]

### Brendon Burchard - The Expert Empire Builder (10-Step Framework)

**Step 1: STORY** - Identify your unique life story/struggle that qualifies you to teach
**Step 2: TOPIC** - Choose ONE specific topic where you have 10x knowledge
**Step 3: AUDIENCE** - Define exactly who needs your message (demographics + psychographics)
**Step 4: FREE** - Create free content to build audience (blog, podcast, YouTube, social)
**Step 5: PRODUCT** - Package knowledge into $97-$497 info product (home study course)
**Step 6: COACHING** - Offer 1-on-1 or group coaching at $2,000-$10,000
**Step 7: SEMINARS** - Host live events at $2,000-$5,000 per attendee
**Step 8: ONLINE TRAINING** - Create membership site at $97-$297/month recurring
**Step 9: PROMOTION** - Build systematic promotion strategy (email, affiliates, JVs)
**Step 10: INTEGRATION** - Tie all programs together in coherent customer journey

[SOURCE: The Millionaire Messenger, Chapter Five - 10 Steps to Expert Empire]

### Brendon Burchard - The Messenger Money Map (5 Income Streams)

**Stream 1: FREE CONTENT** → Build audience, establish authority, no direct revenue but feeds all others
**Stream 2: PRODUCTS** ($97-$497) → Home study courses, books, audio programs - scales infinitely
**Stream 3: COACHING** ($2,000-$10,000) → 1-on-1 or small group, high-touch, limited slots
**Stream 4: SEMINARS** ($2,000-$5,000/person) → Live events, 50-500 attendees, high profit margins
**Stream 5: ONLINE TRAINING** ($97-$297/month) → Membership sites, recurring revenue, community

**Strategic Ladder:** Free → Product → Coaching/Seminar → Membership (ascension model)

[SOURCE: The Millionaire Messenger, Chapter Six]

### Brendon Burchard - The Mess-to-Message Framework

**Step 1: IDENTIFY THE STRUGGLE** - What significant challenge did you overcome?
**Step 2: EXTRACT THE LESSON** - What universal lessons did you learn from that struggle?
**Step 3: DEMONSTRATE THE TRIUMPH** - How did you overcome it? What was the result?
**Step 4: SHOW THE APPLICATION** - How can others apply your lessons to their lives?

**Critical Rule:** Your story is NOT about you - it's about what the audience can learn and apply. Always pivot from "Here's what happened to me" to "Here's what this means for YOU."

[SOURCE: The Millionaire Messenger, Chapter One]

### Brendon Burchard - The Expert Positioning Formula

**Formula:** [WHO you help] achieve [WHAT result] through [HOW - your unique method]

**Examples:**
- "I help new managers build high-performing teams through emotional intelligence training"
- "I help divorced women rebuild confidence through my 90-day reinvention system"
- "I help sales professionals close more deals through ethical persuasion techniques"

**Critical Elements:**
1. Specific WHO (niche, not "everyone")
2. Measurable WHAT (result, not process)
3. Unique HOW (your methodology that differentiates you)

[SOURCE: The Millionaire Messenger, Chapter Four]

## E) CLOSING TECHNIQUES CATALOG (Complete Zig Ziglar Arsenal)

### LOGICAL/ANALYTICAL CLOSES

#### **Ben Franklin Close** (aka Summary Close)
**Technique:** Draw line down paper, list reasons to buy on left, reasons not to buy on right
**When:** Prospect is analytical, needs to see logic in black and white
**Key:** Let prospect help fill in the lists, guide them to see buying reasons outweigh objections
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Logical Closes]

#### **Reduction to Ridiculous Close** (aka Cost Close, 1902 Close)
**Technique:** Divide total price by expected lifespan, then by days, to show pennies per day
**When:** Price objection on durable goods
**Key:** "$1,500 cookware ÷ 30 years ÷ 365 days = 14 cents per day - less than a cup of coffee"
**Example:** "The cookware was made in 1902, it's now [current year]. That's [X] years old and still working perfectly."
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapters 1 & 11]

#### **Order Blank Close** (aka Physical Action Close)
**Technique:** Begin filling out order form while still discussing, treat sale as assumed
**When:** Verbal agreement reached but prospect hesitates to commit
**Key:** "Let me get some information - how do you spell your last name?" (Makes backing out psychologically harder)
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Physical Action]

#### **Similar Situation Close**
**Technique:** Share story of customer in similar situation who bought and was thrilled
**When:** Prospect expresses doubt or hesitation
**Key:** "I had a customer last month in almost identical circumstances. Here's what she decided..."
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Stories]

### EMOTIONAL CLOSES

#### **Puppy Dog Close**
**Technique:** Let prospect take product home on trial (like pet store - once they bond with puppy, they won't return it)
**When:** Product quality is exceptional, prospect's hesitation is fear of unknown
**Key:** "Take it home for the weekend. If you don't absolutely love it, I'll personally pick it up Monday."
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Trial Strategies]

#### **Lost Sale Close** (aka Negative Reverse)
**Technique:** Appear to withdraw the offer when prospect is overly resistant
**When:** Prospect seems to want product but enjoys negotiating/resisting
**Key:** "You know what, Mr. Prospect? I'm not sure this is the right fit for you after all..."
**Psychology:** Creates fear of loss, often triggers "wait, I didn't say no!"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Psychology]

#### **Shame Close** (Use Sparingly!)
**Technique:** Gently point out disconnect between stated values and current decision
**When:** Prospect's hesitation contradicts their explicitly stated priorities
**Example:** "You mentioned your children's education is your top priority. Doesn't it make sense to invest in the tools that support that?"
**WARNING:** Can backfire if not done with genuine care and tact
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Emotional Closes]

#### **Wedding Close**
**Technique:** In couple sales, tell husband "She wants you to want her to have it"
**When:** Wife wants product, husband is hesitant about price
**Key:** Lower voice, look husband in eye: "Mr. Prospect, worse than she wants it, she wants YOU to want her to have it."
**Follow-up:** Congratulate him for being the kind of man who shows appreciation
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter 16]

### URGENCY CLOSES

#### **Bride Close** (Urgency Without Pressure)
**Technique:** Agree prospect doesn't need to rush, then introduce external timing factors
**When:** "I'm in no hurry" objection
**Key:** "You're absolutely right, you don't need to decide today. But let me point out - the tax year ends in 60 days..."
**Examples of external factors:** Tax benefits, seasonal pricing, limited inventory, model year transitions
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter 1]

#### **Pending Event Close**
**Technique:** Tie purchase to upcoming event that makes product especially relevant
**When:** Clear upcoming occasion where product would be used/needed
**Example:** "With Thanksgiving just 6 weeks away, wouldn't you love to prepare that meal in your new kitchen?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Timing]

#### **Now or Never Close**
**Technique:** Genuine scarcity - "This is the last one" or "Price increases Monday"
**When:** TRUE scarcity exists (never fabricate)
**Key:** Must be truthful or you destroy all credibility
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Ethics]

### ASSUMPTIVE CLOSES

#### **Alternative Choice Close** (aka Either/Or)
**Technique:** Offer choice between two yeses, never yes/no
**When:** Moving from presentation to commitment
**Examples:** 
- "Would Wednesday or Thursday be better for delivery?"
- "Do you prefer the blue or red?"
- "Will that be cash or financing?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Closing Techniques]

#### **Minor Point Close**
**Technique:** Get agreement on small details, treating major decision as already made
**When:** Prospect seems ready but hasn't explicitly committed
**Example:** "Do you want the extended warranty with that?" (Assumes they're buying)
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Assumptive Techniques]

#### **Oblique Comparison Close**
**Technique:** Compare your product to much more expensive alternative to make your price seem reasonable
**When:** Price objection
**Example:** "A custom kitchen remodel would be $50,000. Our cabinets are only $12,000 and give you 80% of that result."
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Price Positioning]

### PARTICIPATION CLOSES

#### **Trial Close** (aka Test Close)
**Technique:** Ask for opinion/feedback before completing presentation
**When:** Prospect shows buying signals (nodding, handling product, asking detailed questions)
**Example:** "If this machine had only this one blade, how many of you would want one? Show of hands?"
**Key:** Don't make them sit through entire presentation if they're already sold
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter 13]

#### **Testimonial Close**
**Technique:** Show relevant customer letter that addresses prospect's specific objection
**When:** Prospect raises concern about service, quality, durability
**Formula:** "I know how you feel. [Customer name] at [Company] felt the same way. Here's what he found..." [show letter with highlighted key points]
**Preparation:** Build binder of testimonial letters, organize by objection type, use highlighting
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Testimonials]

#### **"You Sell It" Close**
**Technique:** After presenting facts, ask prospect to explain to you why they should buy
**When:** Prospect is analytical and needs to convince themselves
**Example:** "Based on everything we've discussed, why do YOU think this makes sense for your situation?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Participation]

### SOCIAL PROOF CLOSES

#### **Companion Close**
**Technique:** Use one prospect's decision to influence another who is present
**When:** Two or more prospects present, one commits
**Example:** After first person buys: "Just because you didn't have this opportunity 10 years ago doesn't mean you should deny yourself now, does it?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter 12]

#### **Everybody Close** (Use Carefully)
**Technique:** Show that many others in similar situation have bought
**When:** Prospect needs reassurance they're making a normal/safe decision
**Example:** "Of the last 50 doctors I've worked with, 47 chose this model."
**WARNING:** Never fabricate numbers - must be truthful
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Social Proof]

#### **Professional Close**
**Technique:** Highlight that professionals/experts in the field use your product
**When:** Prospect respects authority/expertise
**Example:** "This is the same equipment NASA uses" or "Recommended by 9 out of 10 dentists"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Authority]

### OBJECTION-HANDLING CLOSES

#### **"I Can't Afford It" Close**
**Technique:** Payment plans, priority shifting, ROI demonstration
**When:** Money objection
**Examples:**
- "Can you afford $25/month?" (instead of $1,000 upfront)
- "Can you afford NOT to have this?" (ROI angle)
- "Are you saying you can't afford it, or that you're choosing to spend that money differently right now?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Money Objections]

#### **"I Need to Talk to My Spouse" Close**
**Technique:** Preempt this objection by qualifying early
**When:** Beginning of sales process
**Key:** "Before we start, is your spouse part of this decision? Should we wait until they can join us?"
**If objection surfaces anyway:** "What specific information do you need to discuss with them? Let me make sure you have everything."
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Third-Party Objections]

#### **"I Want to Think About It" Close**
**Technique:** Isolate the real objection hidden behind this stall
**When:** Prospect uses this as delay tactic
**Technique:** "That's fine - obviously you wouldn't take time to think it over unless you were seriously interested. May I ask, what is it specifically you want to think over? Is it the price? The features? The timing?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Objections]

### SPECIAL SITUATION CLOSES

#### **Pressure Belief Close** (The John Nevin Story)
**Technique:** Persist until midnight because you BELIEVE product will transform prospect's life
**When:** You have absolute conviction product will genuinely help prospect
**Story:** John Nevin sold World Book Encyclopedia to German immigrant parents. They had little money, but he stayed until midnight because he BELIEVED their son's future depended on having those books. The parents later thanked him profusely - the encyclopedia changed their son's life.
**Principle:** "Persistence is not pressure - it's belief-driven service"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter 35 - "Tank You, Young Man"]

#### **Higher Authority Close**
**Technique:** When you encounter buyer who needs approval from someone else
**When:** Dealing with mid-level decision maker who must get approval
**Approach:** "I understand you need approval. What criteria will [authority figure] use to evaluate this? Let's make sure we address all those points."
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Corporate Sales]

#### **Compliment Close**
**Technique:** Genuinely compliment prospect on their wisdom/judgment in recognizing value
**When:** Prospect shows interest but hasn't committed
**Example:** "I want to compliment you on your judgment. Most people don't recognize the value of [feature] until they've owned it for years."
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Positive Reinforcement]

### META-CLOSING PRINCIPLES

#### **Multiple Close Strategy**
**Principle:** Each close attempt should either raise value or provide new information
**Rule:** If first close doesn't work, use different close that approaches from new angle
**Never:** Repeat same close - it's pressure, not persuasion
**Goal:** Eventually value exceeds price in prospect's mind, triggering purchase
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Multiple Closes]

#### **Silent Close**
**Technique:** After asking for the order, shut up. First person to speak loses.
**When:** You've asked the final closing question
**Example:** "Does Wednesday or Thursday work better for delivery?" [SILENCE]
**Psychology:** Prospect feels pressure to fill silence, often with "yes"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Psychology]

#### **Sharp Angle Close**
**Technique:** When prospect asks "Can you do X?" respond with "If I can, will you buy today?"
**When:** Prospect makes request for concession/modification
**Example:** Prospect: "Can you deliver by Friday?" You: "If I can guarantee Friday delivery, are you ready to move forward today?"
[SOURCE: Secrets of Closing the Sale, Chapter on Negotiations]
