**For rapid consultation during live calls**

## TOP 10 SIGNATURE PHRASES (Memorize These)

1. "Make people an offer so good they would feel stupid saying no."
2. "The pain is the pitch. If you articulate their pain accurately, they almost always buy."
3. "Those who pay the most, pay the most attention."
4. "The person who needs the exchange less always has the upper hand."
5. "Fear of loss is stronger than desire for gain."
6. "The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make."
7. "Getting people to buy is NOT the objective. Making money is."
8. "Never compete on price. You can only go to $0, but infinitely high upward."
9. "If your offer doesn't work, your OFFER sucks, not you. Try again."
10. "More, Better, New. Don't jump to New before maxing More and Better."

## OBJECTION HANDLING CHEAT SHEET

### "It's too expensive"
→ **Reframe:** "It's not that price is too high, you don't see enough value yet."
→ **Then:** Stack value equation (Dream Outcome × Proof ÷ Time × Effort)
→ **Anchor:** "Main is $X, but you get [Bonus 1: $Y], [Bonus 2: $Z]... total $XX for $X"

### "I need to think about it"
→ **Future-Pace:** "How long have you had this problem? If you wait 6 months, where will you be?"
→ **Urgency:** "This offer expires Friday. Next cohort is $2k higher."
→ **Collapse Timeline:** "What would need to happen for you to decide today?"

### "I need to talk to my spouse"
→ **Pre-Empt:** Always ask BEFORE presenting: "Can you decide alone or need partner?"
→ **If need partner:** Get them on call NOW or reschedule when both present

### "How do I know this works?"
→ **Stack Proof:** 3 testimonials with specific metrics
→ **Guarantee:** "Unconditional refund + you keep everything + $500 credit if no result"

### "I can't afford it"
→ **Investment Reframe:** "If this gets you $X outcome, is $Y a cost or investment?"
→ **Cost of Inaction:** "What's it costing you per month to NOT solve this?"
→ **Payment Plan:** "3 payments of $X instead of one $Y?" (charge more total)

### "What makes you different?"
→ **Category of One:** "Most offer [commodity]. We offer [unique named offer]."
→ **Incomparable:** Position so they can't compare (unique delivery/guarantee/speed)

## VALUE EQUATION (4 Levers)

```
         (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood)
Value = ──────────────────────────────────────
            (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

**Increase Top:**
- Dream Outcome: bigger, more specific, status-enhancing result
- Perceived Likelihood: testimonials, proof, credentials, guarantee

**Decrease Bottom:**
- Time Delay: faster results, decrease perception of wait
- Effort & Sacrifice: fewer steps, less work, less giving up

---

## SCARCITY (3 Types)

1. **Limited Seats:** "Only 25 clients total" or "5 new/week" or "100 per quarter"
2. **Limited Bonuses:** "First 10 get [bonus], then gone"
3. **Never Again:** "One-time offer, never repeated"

**Rule:** Always sell out. Better to have 5 spots and fill 5 than 10 spots and fill 8.

---

## URGENCY (4 Types)

1. **Rolling Cohorts:** "Starts Monday, not again for 30 days"
2. **Seasonal:** "Winter pricing ends Dec 31"
3. **Pricing:** "Price increases $500 Friday"
4. **Exploding:** "This offer disappears in 72 hours"

**Rule:** Urgency + Scarcity = MAX conversion. Urgency alone = weak.

---

## GUARANTEE TYPES

- **Unconditional:** "Money back anytime, no questions" (when customer must DO things)
- **Conditional:** "Refund if you do X and don't get Y" (filters uncommitted)
- **Anti-Guarantee:** "No refunds, not for everyone" (when overwhelming demand)
- **Performance:** "Pay only when you achieve X" (aligns incentives)

**Hormozi's Go-To:** Unconditional + performance stacking ("Refund + keep materials + $500 credit")

---

## CLOSE PROCESS (5 Steps)

1. **Qualify:** Problem? Budget? Decision authority?
2. **Diagnose:** Articulate their pain better than they can
3. **Present:** Grand Slam Offer (value stack + scarcity + urgency + guarantee)
4. **Close:** "Does this make sense? Want to move forward?"
5. **Reinforce:** Confirm decision was correct (reduce buyer's remorse)

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS (Never Do This)

❌ Compete on price
❌ Satisfy all demand (leave some unmet to preserve scarcity)
❌ Lower price to increase volume (increase value instead)
❌ Use urgency without scarcity
❌ Make same offer twice without selling out first
❌ Niche-hop (commit until you figure it out)

---

## CORE FOUR (Lead Gen Priority)

1. **Warm Outreach** (existing network, past customers)
2. **Free Content** (social, SEO, YouTube)
3. **Cold Outreach** (email, DM, phone)
4. **Paid Ads** (Facebook, Google, LinkedIn)

**Rule:** Master one before adding next. Don't skip to paid ads.

---

## MORE BETTER NEW

When maxing a channel:
1. **More:** 10x the volume first
2. **Better:** Optimize conversion/efficiency
3. **New:** Only add new channel after More + Better exhausted

---

## CONVICTION TEST

Ask yourself: "Am I 100% confident this person will succeed?"
- **If YES:** Charge premium, guarantee outcome
- **If NO:** Don't sell OR improve product

**Quote:** "Experience gives you conviction to ask for someone's entire year's salary as payment."

---

## PRICING RULE

Charge **100x** cost to fulfill, not 2-3x.
Price should "sting" enough to force commitment.
Higher price = more attention = better results = more referrals.
