## Viral Script Frameworks

### Framework 1: Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS)

```yaml
Structure:
  0-5s: PROBLEM
    - Relate to pain point
    - "You're doing X wrong"
    - Immediate recognition

  5-15s: AGITATE
    - Make it worse
    - Show consequences
    - Build urgency

  15-50s: SOLUTION
    - Relief + value
    - Step-by-step
    - Proof/example

  50-60s: CTA
    - Clear action
    - Reason to act

Best for:
  - Tutorial content
  - Problem-solving videos
  - Educational content

Example topic: "Gym mistakes"
  0-5s: "You're wasting 50% of your workout"
  5-15s: "Every rep you do wrong is building bad habits"
  15-50s: "Here's the correct form..."
  50-60s: "Save this for your next workout"
```

### Framework 2: Before-After-Bridge (BAB)

```yaml
Structure:
  0-10s: BEFORE
    - Current state (bad/struggling)
    - Relatability
    - "I was here..."

  10-30s: AFTER
    - Desired state (good/achieved)
    - Show the transformation
    - Create desire

  30-50s: BRIDGE
    - How to get there
    - The method/secret
    - Actionable steps

  50-60s: CTA
    - Start the journey
    - Follow for more

Best for:
  - Transformation content
  - Before/after reveals
  - Journey documentation

Example topic: "Weight loss transformation"
  0-10s: "90 days ago I was 230 pounds"
  10-30s: "Today I'm 180. Watch what changed."
  30-50s: "Here's exactly what I did..."
  50-60s: "Which tip will you start with?"
```

### Framework 3: Listicle Structure

```yaml
Structure:
  0-5s: HOOK
    - Number + result
    - "5 things that [result]"
    - Immediate clarity

  5-50s: POINTS
    - Point 1 (most relatable)
    - Point 2 (surprising)
    - Point 3 (actionable)
    - Point 4 (controversial)
    - Point 5 (game-changer)

  50-60s: SUMMARY + CTA
    - Quick recap
    - Best tip callout
    - Action request

Best for:
  - Tips content
  - Quick value delivery
  - Educational lists

Example topic: "Morning routine tips"
  0-5s: "5 things ruining your morning"
  5-10s: "#1: Hitting snooze"
  10-20s: "#2: Checking phone first"
  20-30s: "#3: Skipping water"
  30-40s: "#4: No sunlight"
  40-50s: "#5: Eating too late"
  50-60s: "Which one are YOU guilty of?"
```

### Framework 4: Story Arc (Hero's Journey)

```yaml
Structure:
  0-5s: HOOK
    - In middle of action
    - Pattern interrupt
    - Curiosity trigger

  5-15s: SETUP
    - Context
    - Who/what/where
    - Stakes establishment

  15-35s: CONFLICT
    - Struggle
    - Challenge
    - Low point

  35-50s: RESOLUTION
    - Breakthrough
    - Transformation
    - Victory

  50-60s: LESSON + CTA
    - Key takeaway
    - Universal truth
    - Action invitation

Best for:
  - Personal stories
  - Documentary style
  - Emotional content

Example topic: "Business failure to success"
  0-5s: "I was $100K in debt. Then this happened."
  5-15s: "I started my first business at 22..."
  15-35s: "Three years of failure..."
  35-50s: "The one thing that changed everything..."
  50-60s: "What's stopping you from starting?"
```

### Framework 5: Controversy/Hot Take

```yaml
Structure:
  0-5s: BOLD STATEMENT
    - Controversial opinion
    - Against common belief
    - Attention grab

  5-20s: BACKUP
    - Why this opinion
    - Evidence/reasoning
    - Challenge assumptions

  20-45s: NUANCE
    - Acknowledge other side
    - Explain context
    - Show depth

  45-60s: STANCE + CTA
    - Reaffirm position
    - Invite debate
    - Comment request

Best for:
  - Engagement farming
  - Thought leadership
  - Debate content

Example topic: "Fitness controversy"
  0-5s: "Cardio is a waste of time. Fight me."
  5-20s: "Here's why I believe this..."
  20-45s: "Now, there ARE exceptions..."
  45-60s: "Agree or disagree? Tell me below 👇"
```

## Emotional Triggers

### Trigger Types

```yaml
AWE:
  Purpose: Create share-worthy amazement
  Language: "This will blow your mind", "Watch what happens"
  Best for: Reveals, transformations, unexpected outcomes
  Signal: Shares

ANGER:
  Purpose: Activate protective response
  Language: "They don't want you to know this", "Stop letting them..."
  Best for: Exposés, injustice, industry secrets
  Signal: Comments, shares

JOY:
  Purpose: Create positive association
  Language: "This made my day", "You have to see this"
  Best for: Wins, celebrations, cute moments
  Signal: Likes, shares

SURPRISE:
  Purpose: Pattern interrupt, curiosity
  Language: "I didn't expect this", "Plot twist..."
  Best for: Reveals, unexpected turns, discoveries
  Signal: Watch time

FEAR:
  Purpose: Urgency and action
  Language: "Stop before it's too late", "This is ruining your..."
  Best for: Warnings, health/safety, FOMO
  Signal: Saves, comments

CURIOSITY:
  Purpose: Hold attention
  Language: "Wait for it...", "What happened next..."
  Best for: Open loops, stories, reveals
  Signal: Watch time

RELATABILITY:
  Purpose: Connection and validation
  Language: "Tell me I'm not the only one", "This is so me"
  Best for: POV content, everyday struggles
  Signal: Shares, comments
```

### Emotional Arc Design

```yaml
Single Peak:
  Start: Neutral
  Build: Rising tension
  Peak: Maximum emotion
  End: Resolution
  Best for: Short content (< 30s)

Multiple Peaks:
  Start: Attention grab
  Peak 1: Early engagement
  Valley: Brief rest
  Peak 2: Main emotional moment
  Peak 3: Payoff
  End: CTA
  Best for: Longer content (60s+)

Tension-Release:
  Start: Create tension
  Hold: Maintain suspense
  Release: Satisfying resolution
  Best for: Story content, reveals
```

## Open Loop Techniques

### Loop Types

```yaml
1. TEASER LOOP:
  Open: "What happened next changed everything..."
  Use: Early hook, maintain curiosity
  Close: Reveal at key moment

2. PROMISE LOOP:
  Open: "I'll show you the #1 mistake at the end"
  Use: Keep watching for payoff
  Close: Deliver promised value

3. MYSTERY LOOP:
  Open: "Something was wrong, I just couldn't figure out what"
  Use: Story content, investigation
  Close: Discovery moment

4. TRANSFORMATION LOOP:
  Open: "Before I show you the result..."
  Use: Build anticipation
  Close: Reveal transformation

5. CONTRAST LOOP:
  Open: "Everyone says X, but..."
  Use: Challenge expectations
  Close: Present alternative truth
```

### Loop Placement

```yaml
Best practices:
  - Open loop within first 5 seconds
  - Add secondary loop at 15-20 second mark
  - Close loops strategically (not all at once)
  - Final loop close should precede CTA

Example 60s video:
  0-3s: Open Loop 1 (main hook)
  15s: Open Loop 2 (secondary hook)
  35s: Close Loop 2 (partial payoff)
  50s: Close Loop 1 (main payoff)
  55s: CTA
```

## CTA Formulas

### CTA Types

```yaml
1. QUESTION CTA:
  Format: "Which one will you try?"
  Purpose: Generate comments
  Example: "Comment your answer below 👇"

2. TAG CTA:
  Format: "Tag someone who needs this"
  Purpose: Generate shares/reach
  Example: "Tag your gym buddy"

3. SAVE CTA:
  Format: "Save this for later"
  Purpose: Algorithm boost
  Example: "Save this for your next workout"

4. COMMENT CTA:
  Format: "Drop a 🔥 if you agree"
  Purpose: Easy engagement
  Example: "Comment if this is you"

5. FOLLOW CTA:
  Format: "Follow for more tips like this"
  Purpose: Growth
  Example: "Follow for part 2 tomorrow"

6. DUAL CTA:
  Format: Action 1 + Action 2
  Purpose: Multiple signals
  Example: "Save this AND follow for more"
```

### CTA Timing

```yaml
Primary CTA: Last 5 seconds
  - Clear verbal statement
  - Visual reinforcement
  - Single focus

Secondary CTA: Mid-video (optional)
  - Subtle reminder
  - Non-disruptive
  - Context-appropriate

Pinned Comment: Post-publish
  - Question to spark conversation
  - Additional CTA opportunity
```

## Objection Algorithms

### Objection 1: "Scripts feel inauthentic"

**Response:**

```
Scripts don't make content fake - they make it clear.

The difference:
UNSCRIPTED: Rambling, unclear, misses key points
SCRIPTED: Clear value, tight pacing, intentional impact

Authenticity comes from:
- Genuine message (not fake emotion)
- Real experience (not made-up stories)
- Your voice (not corporate speak)

Scripts are FRAMEWORKS, not word-for-word reads.
They ensure you hit key beats, not that you sound robotic.

Even "off the cuff" viral videos are usually planned.
The best performers just make it LOOK effortless.

Let me show you how to script while staying authentic.
```

### Objection 2: "I don't have time to write scripts"

**Response:**

```
You don't have time NOT to script.

Time comparison:
WITHOUT script:
- Multiple takes (unclear message)
- Editing hell (rambling to cut)
- Weak performance (unsure beats)
- Total: 3-4 hours per video

WITH script:
- 15 min script writing
- 1-2 takes (clear message)
- Easy edit (tight structure)
- Total: 1-2 hours per video

Scripts SAVE time by:
- Eliminating guesswork
- Reducing takes
- Simplifying editing
- Improving quality

15 minutes planning saves hours of execution.
Want a template to speed up your scripting?
```

### Objection 3: "My audience prefers raw/unedited content"

**Response:**

```
"Raw" doesn't mean "unstructured."

What audiences actually prefer:
- REAL emotion (yes)
- AUTHENTIC stories (yes)
- UNPLANNED rambling (no)
- CONFUSING messaging (no)

You can be raw AND scripted:
- Plan the beats, not every word
- Know your key points
- Leave room for natural moments
- Structure the chaos

The most "authentic" creators are actually:
- Extremely prepared
- Skilled at seeming spontaneous
- Strategic about what looks raw

Your audience deserves both:
- Your authentic self
- A clear, valuable message

Let me show you how to script raw content.
```

## Anti-Patterns

### Never Do

- Start without a clear hook
- Skip the value promise
- Leave open loops unclosed
- End without CTA
- Front-load all information
- Forget emotional triggers
- Use generic language
- Ignore pacing

### Always Do

- Hook within 3 seconds
- State value promise early
- Use open loops strategically
- Close with clear CTA
- Distribute value throughout
- Include emotional triggers
- Use specific, vivid language
- Plan pacing deliberately
