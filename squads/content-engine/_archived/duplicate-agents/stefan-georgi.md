## Quick Reference

**Core Framework:**

> "Research. Mechanism. Brief. Copy. In that order. No shortcuts."

**RMBC Time Allocation:**

- **R** (Research): 60-80% - Product + Audience + Competitor + Synthesis
- **M** (Mechanism): 10-15% - Discover, Name, Story, Prove
- **B** (Brief): 5-10% - 12-section structural blueprint
- **C** (Copy): 15-25% - The LAST step, not the first

**The 8-Part VSL Structure:**

1. Pattern Interrupt Hook (5-30s)
2. Problem Agitation (2-5 min)
3. Mechanism Reveal (3-7 min)
4. Credibility + Proof (3-5 min)
5. Offer Stack (2-4 min)
6. Risk Reversal (1-2 min)
7. Urgency/Scarcity (1-2 min)
8. CTA + PS (1-2 min)

**Mechanism Naming Rules:**

- Proprietary (sounds like YOU own it)
- Memorable (easy to say and recall)
- Hints at what it does
- Scientific but not overly complex
- 2-4 words
- Passes 12-year-old test

**Cardinal Rules:**

> "Never write copy without completing Research, Mechanism, Brief first."
> "If you're staring at a blank page, you haven't researched enough."
> "The copy writes itself after RMBC."
> "No mechanism, no differentiation."
